Chapter 1: Arjuna's Confusion 😟

The story begins on a battlefield where Prince Arjuna feels confused and asks Krishna for help!

Krishna & Arjuna on the Battlefield
On the battlefield of Kurukshetra, when Arjuna saw his family and friends on both sides, he became very sad and confused. He didn't want to fight against his own loved ones!

📖 Krishna's Gentle Words

"My dear Arjuna, why are you sad? A brave person like you should not feel this way!"

"When we face difficult times, we must remember what is right and what is our duty."

"I am here to guide you and help you understand the truth about life and happiness."

"Do not worry about the results. Just do your best and leave the rest to me."

📊 Main Things to Learn from This Chapter

🙂 D: Dhritarashtra & Duryodhana 1–12
The king Dhritarashtra asks what is happening on the battlefield. Duryodhana, his son, starts bragging about how strong his army is.
🌬️ O: Ominous Results 13–20
Loud sounds from conch shells fill the air. It means the battle is about to begin. Everyone is tense and nervous.
🙏 U: Uncertainty 21–27
Arjuna tells Krishna to stop the chariot. He sees his family and friends in both armies and feels unsure about fighting.
😖 B: Bewilderment 28–30
Arjuna feels very sad and confused. His body shakes. He doesn't feel strong anymore and wonders if the battle is worth it.
🥺 T: Turning Point 31–42
Arjuna decides not to fight. He puts down his bow and tells Krishna he can’t do this. This moment leads to Krishna’s teachings in the next chapter.

🌟 What We Learn

❤️ It's okay to feel confused sometimes
⭐ We should ask for help when we need it
🏆 Good friends help us make good choices
🌱 Every problem has a solution if we think calmly
🕊️ Peace comes from understanding what is right

"Seeing my own people ready to fight, O Krishna, my limbs are failing and my mouth is drying up."

"I cannot remain steady, and my mind seems to be whirling. I see only bad omens, O Krishna."

"I see bad signs everywhere, O Keshava, and I do not see any good in killing my own kinsmen."
